Bio

Bio

Hey there! Welcome to my website.

I'm Borna Khodabandeh, an undergraduate student in Electrical Engineering at Sharif University of Technology with a minor in Physics.

During my undergraduate studies, I had the privilege of collaborating with Professor Michael Unser at EPFL on designing robust, efficient and energy preserving neural networks for inverse problems Professor Sajjad Amini and Professor Moosavi-Dezfooli on adversarial robustness in contrastive learning. I also had the opportunity to work with Professor Arash Amini on various topics.

Currently, I'm exploring exciting research areas such as:

  • Preference Optimization and Reinforcement Learning: Developing methods to align AI systems with human preferences. Learning from more than just raw data and moving away from reward-based RL.
  • Deep Learning and Optimization: Enhancing the efficiency, robustness and explainability of machine learning models. Geometric methods in optimization.
  • Game-Theoretic Approaches in AI: Using game theoric approaches in AI, beyond equilibrium learning. developing computational models.
  • Information Theoric and high dimensional statistic approaches: understanding the strengths and limitation of models using statistical approaches. Understanding the statistical behavior of systems.
  • Graphical models, learning on graphs, using graphs

Beyond research, I’m passionate about education and mentoring. I’ve led a research group and conducted practice sessions for courses Here is a repository of my recorded sessions. I believe in the power of knowledge sharing and collaboration.

Looking ahead, I aspire to pursue a PhD, focusing on interdisciplinary research that integrates machine learning, optimization, and social choice theory. My goal is to develop AI systems that are transparent, efficient, and aligned with human values, ultimately aiming to address significant societal challenges through innovative solutions.
